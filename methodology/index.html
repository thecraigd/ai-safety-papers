Powered by Google Gemini

All paper analysis is performed by Google Gemini. The model reads each paper's title and abstract to determine AI safety relevance, assign categories, generate summaries, and score papers on multiple quality dimensions.

We use an "LLM-as-judge" approach, which enables consistent, scalable curation while being transparent about the selection process. The model evaluates hundreds of papers daily that would be impossible to review manually.

We currently use Google Gemini because it offers an excellent balance of capability and cost efficiency for this task. As a community resource, we continuously monitor which AI service provides the best value for the AI safety research community, and may adjust our approach as the landscape evolves.

Editor Scoring System

Each paper is evaluated on five dimensions that matter most for AI safety research. These scores determine which papers appear in "Today's Top Papers" and the weekly Editor's Choice digest.

Evaluation Dimensions

35% Significance

How important is this for AI safety? Does it address a critical problem or open new research directions?

25% Novelty

How original is the contribution? Does it introduce new concepts or significantly advance existing ones?

20% Technical Quality

How rigorous is the methodology? Are claims well-supported by evidence?

15% Timeliness

How relevant is this to current AI development? Does it address emerging risks or capabilities?

5% Accessibility

How readable for a broad ML audience? Can practitioners apply these insights?

// Weighted score formula

weighted_score = significance × 0.35 + novelty × 0.25 + quality × 0.20 + timeliness × 0.15 + accessibility × 0.05

Papers are scored from 1-10 on each dimension. The weighted score ranks papers for daily and weekly highlights. This approach enables consistent, scalable curation while being fully transparent about selection criteria.

AI Safety Taxonomy

Papers are automatically assigned to one or more of our 6 broad categories, plus fine-grained tags for more specific topic discovery.

Categories

Interpretability

Understanding how models work internally - circuits, features, representations, mechanistic analysis

Alignment & Learning

Training AI to do what we want - RLHF, preference learning, value alignment, reward modeling, goal specification

Robustness & Security

Adversarial attacks, jailbreaking, prompt injection, defenses, content filtering, guardrails

Evaluations

Measuring AI capabilities or safety - benchmarks, red-teaming, safety evals, capability assessments

Agents & Control

Autonomous AI systems, tool use, human oversight, controllability, corrigibility, multi-agent safety

Policy & Governance

AI regulation, deployment practices, societal impact, risk assessment, position papers, policy proposals

Fine-Grained Tags

In addition to broad categories, each paper receives multiple fine-grained tags from a curated taxonomy of 51 specific topics. These tags enable more precise filtering and discovery across areas like:

jailbreaking sparse-autoencoders reward-hacking red-teaming tool-use corrigibility constitutional-ai and more...

New tags are proposed by the AI during paper review and can be added after human approval, allowing the taxonomy to evolve with the field.

Transparency & Limitations

We believe in being upfront about what our automated system can and cannot do:

  • • Abstract-only analysis: All evaluations are based on titles and abstracts only, not full paper content. Important nuances in the full paper may be missed.
  • • AI-generated content: Summaries and scores are generated by Gemini. While generally accurate, they may contain errors or miss key points.
  • • Imperfect filtering: Some relevant papers may be missed (false negatives), and some less relevant papers may be included (false positives).
  • • Processing delays: Papers typically appear 24-48 hours after arXiv submission due to arXiv's own processing times.
  • • Not a quality judgment: Scores reflect estimated relevance and impact, not objective assessment of research quality. Always read papers yourself before citing.

See something we missed? Have suggestions for improvement? Get in touch.

Open Data

We believe AI safety research benefits from open access to curated datasets. Our complete paper index is freely available for download and use in your own research, analysis, or tooling.

What's included

  • • Full metadata for every paper in our index (titles, authors, abstracts, arXiv URLs)
  • • AI-generated summaries and key contributions
  • • Category and tag assignments across our safety taxonomy
  • • Editor scores (novelty, significance, quality, timeliness, accessibility)
  • • Updated daily as new papers are processed
JSON format

Use this data for meta-analyses, research trend tracking, building safety-focused tools, or any purpose that advances AI safety. If you build something with it, we'd love to hear about it.
